Course Specification: Ethics in Human-Computer Interaction

General Information

Course Title: Ethics in Human-Computer Interaction

Course Code: HCI 2204

Credit Hours: 2 (Lecture: 30 contact hours)

Course Type: Department — Required

Level/Year: 2nd Year / Level 4

Course Description: This course examines the ethical considerations that arise in human-computer interaction, focusing on moral principles, privacy, intellectual property, and social responsibilities. Students analyze ethical dilemmas and frameworks, explore inclusivity and accessibility, and evaluate societal impacts of HCI practices and emerging technologies.

Program: BSc in Human Computer Interaction

Department: Software Engineering

College: Computing

Institution: Umm Al Qura University

Version: 1.0

Last Revision Date: 22/04/2025

Course Learning Outcomes

Code Outcome Description
1.1 Understand the fundamental principles of Human‑Computer Interaction.
1.2 Apply usability principles to design effective user interfaces.
1.3 Evaluate user interfaces based on usability and accessibility standards.

Course Topics

  1. Introduction to Ethics in HCI
  2. Ethical Frameworks and Theories
  3. Privacy, Security, and Civil Liberties
  4. Intellectual Property and Software Ethics
  5. Social Implications of Computing
  6. Inclusivity, Accessibility, and Cultural Contexts
  7. Professional Responsibility in HCI
  8. Emerging Technologies and Ethical Challenges
  9. Case Studies in HCI Ethics
  10. Ethical Research and Experimentation
